ARH 131 - Survey of Western Art. It’s super chill. Three exams, take home + online, noncumulative. Weekly 2-4 5-15min YouTube videos to summarize in 125 words is the only work + 1 short project. Workload isn’t bad at all. Attendance is taken but I actually enjoy the lectures and breeze through exams by going to class and listening. KIN202 - Applied Nutrition for Health and Performance. If you’re interested in preventative medicine/nutrition take Wesley Smith. I personally enjoy his lectures and learn a lot about nutrition for our health. It’s a basic level class, no chemistry or biology required. Quizzes are take home and online. A few in class assignments here and there randomly for attendance purposes - no attendance taken otherwise. 3 exams, no final, third exam is noncumulative during finals week online. No projects.

BUS201 - Money. It’s essentially a personal finance class with more information on taxes and insurance than I learned in high school and honestly didn’t know anything about before this class. Professor Manuel Sicre wants you to come to class and learn and ask plenty of questions. I enjoy his lectures and he engages in conversation a lot, there’s no stupid questions. Attendance is taken every class. Going to class practically guarantees you a 90% and above on every exam. 2 exams (noncumulative) 30 MCQs each. A cumulative 55 MCQ exam for the final. There’s chapter homework, 10 MCQ questions, due before each exam on whatever chapters the exam is on. To prepare for exams he creates practice tests we do in class and can ask questions and review and also a study guide. Professor Sicre actually wants you to learn since he is teaching you about real life things, if you aren’t going to go to class it’s not worth taking.
